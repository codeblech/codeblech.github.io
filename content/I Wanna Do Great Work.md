I have, by coincidence(?), spent much of my time building on top of web technologies. Though, I have not built or contributed to any foundational or fundamental technology there, I feel that this is the area/domain of knowledge where I am closest to the frontier of knowledge/engineering.

I am familiar to cloud engineering too, but have not really worked on anything of substance here. Sure, I know the common jargon and have worked on a few tools and technologies. But, I haven't built anything of value here, unlike I have in the web world. Maybe building the IDP at Sprinklr on top of [Backstage](https://backstage.io/) counts, but I'm totally unimpressed by that.

I am extremely intrigued by CPUs, GPUs, accelerators, assembly, ISAs, chip design, RISC-V, compilers, LLVM, MLIR, and rest of everything [Modular](https://www.modular.com) works on. Today, this field seems to be very intertwined with the development of AI, and growth of the Machine Learning domain. The Machine Learning domain might be darkest of black holes in my knowledge. Though, I know bits and pieces of how it works on a very, very high level.

It seems like, I am most likely to get a day job that involves building something on top of web technologies and/or "the cloud". I will try to contribute to the fundamental technologies at play there, and not just build on top of them. This is to not discount the engineering discipline required to build on top of the web/cloud. I know that is in itself a very cool thing. I enjoyed building [JPortal](https://github.com/codeblech/jportal), and enjoyed doing little K8s things at Sprinklr. But, I want to do more. I want to build the Kubernetes. I want to build Pyodide, FastAPI, and maybe even Python itself. I want to know what Type systems are. I want to know how and why are programming languages different in the ways they are. I want to be able to do everything [Lusion.co](https://lusion.co) does. All of this is very near to what all I have been working on. I just need to start looking what's underneath the shoulders of these giants.

The web and the cloud world is relatively easier for me to explore more because I have some idea of the map of knowledge here. A little more difficult, and much more intriguing for me is everything else. Maybe because it is the shiny new thing for me – Machine Learning, Compilers, Graphics Programming, AI inference, sandboxes, AI Harnesses, everything [Modular](https://www.modular.com) does. I am not very sure what I would do that'd be of value in these fields. But that'd become obvious once I learn more and get to the frontiers of knowledge.
